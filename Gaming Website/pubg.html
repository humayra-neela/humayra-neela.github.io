What is PUBG?

PlayerUnknown’s Battlegrounds (PUBG) is a battle royale game developed by PUBG Studios and published by Krafton. It popularized the genre, dropping players onto an island where they must scavenge for weapons and eliminate others to be the last one standing.

Gameplay Mechanics

Players parachute onto a vast map and must loot buildings for weapons, armor, and supplies. The playable area gradually shrinks, forcing players into closer combat. Strategic thinking, positioning, and quick reflexes are crucial for survival.

Maps and Modes

PUBG features multiple diverse maps like Erangel, Miramar, Sanhok, Vikendi, and Deston. Each has unique environments and gameplay dynamics. Modes include solo, duo, and squad — plus ranked and arcade options.

Development & History

PUBG was first released in early access in March 2017. Brendan "PlayerUnknown" Greene developed it based on ARMA 2 mods. It quickly became a global sensation, breaking Steam player count records and influencing countless other games.

Global Popularity

PUBG has a massive player base worldwide, especially in countries like India, South Korea, and China. Its mobile version, PUBG Mobile, has expanded its reach even further, becoming one of the highest-grossing mobile games ever.

Esports and Competitive Scene

PUBG has hosted numerous international tournaments such as the PUBG Global Invitational and PUBG Continental Series. Its esports ecosystem emphasizes survival skills, strategy, and precision.
